# The Bisesero Hills: Environmental & Narrative Design

## Historical Context
The Bisesero Hills in Rwanda's Western Province became a symbol of resistance during the 1994 genocide. Unlike many areas where people fled, Tutsis in Bisesero organized collective self-defense using stones, spears, and the terrain's natural advantages. They held the heights for weeks against armed militias—a rare instance of organized resistance.

## Physical Geography

### Vertical Storytelling
The hills use **elevation as narrative**:
- **Low Ground (200m)**: The approach; where threats originate; the lake shore where escape routes began
- **Mid-Level (400-600m)**: Settlements, family compounds, hiding places; where choices were made
- **Heights (700m+)**: The "Hill of Resistance"—the last sanctuary where people made final stands

### Key Locations

#### 1. **The Family Compound (Mid-Level)**
- Overgrown stone foundations
- A well with a partially hidden cellar entrance
- Eucalyptus grove providing cover and concealment
- Scattered family artifacts (pottery, tools)
- **Narrative Role**: Where players begin; where the ledger is hidden

#### 2. **The Lake Shore (Low Ground)**
- Rocky beach with weathered boat infrastructure
- Mist rolling in from the water at dawn/dusk
- Dense reeds and hiding spots
- A half-submerged dock from 1994
- **Narrative Role**: The escape route; where boats departed; haunting emptiness

#### 3. **The Cellar (Underground)**
- Stone-walled, accessed through the family well
- Cramped, dark, with evidence of long habitation
- Preserved food stores, sleeping mats, a journal
- **Narrative Role**: The Hider Path; finding this unlocks Choice A

#### 4. **The Ravine (Mid-to-High Level)**
- A steep cleft in the hills with a narrow path down
- Dense mist; difficult visibility (contributes to puzzle mechanics)
- Scattered stones used as weapons/barricades
- **Narrative Role**: The site of resistance; evidence of conflict

#### 5. **The Height (High Ground)**
- Open rocky plateau with views across the region
- Wind-swept, sparse vegetation
- Remnants of stone fortifications
- **Narrative Role**: The final stand; perspective and reflection

## Atmospheric Design

### Visual Palette
- **Eucalyptus greens** (dense, shadowed)
- **Red earth and weathered stone** (age, abandonment)
- **Gray mist** (from lake; concealment, uncertainty)
- **Deep blues** of Lake Kivu (both beautiful and ominous)

### Dynamic Atmosphere
- **Early Morning**: Heavy mist obscures lower elevations; visibility improves at heights
- **Midday**: Clear views, strong shadows; heat haze off the lake
- **Dusk**: Golden light turning red; mist returns
- **Night**: Starlight only; sounds emphasize isolation

### Ambient Sounds
- Wind through eucalyptus (constant, meditative)
- Water lapping at shore (persistence of nature)
- Birds (sparse, emphasizing emptiness)
- No human voices (absence as presence)
- Distant sounds carrying across valleys (unease)

## Environmental Storytelling

### Evidence Layers
Players discover the story through **layered evidence**, not dialogue:

1. **Physical remains**: Pottery, tools, clothing
2. **Architecture**: Hidden spaces, defensible positions, escape routes
3. **Nature's reclamation**: Vines through windows, trees through floors
4. **Graffiti/Marks**: Scratched dates, names, symbols
5. **Arranged objects**: Items deliberately hidden or placed

### The Ledger
The shepherd's ledger is fragmented across locations:
- **Main journal**: Hidden in the cellar
- **Field notes**: Scattered in hiding spots (corral, ravine, shore)
- **Maps/Sketches**: Showing routes, numbers, locations
- **Personal items**: Letters, photographs (contextualizing choices)

## Traversal Challenges

### Natural Puzzles
The environment itself becomes puzzle-like:
- **Mist**: Obscures paths; navigate by memory/sound
- **Elevation**: Reaching heights requires finding secure routes
- **Loose stone**: Paths crumble; must choose stable footing
- **Overgrowth**: Dense vines block direct routes; requires detours
- **Water**: Lake crossing feels final; one-way points of no return

### Accessibility & Pacing
- **Lower areas**: More straightforward; easier navigation
- **Mid-level**: Moderate difficulty; multiple paths converge
- **Heights**: Challenging terrain; rewards exploration and observation

## Narrative Transitions Between Areas

### Moving Down (Toward the Lake)
*Emotional tone: Desperation, finality, escape*
- Air cools as altitude drops
- Mist thickens; visibility decreases
- Sound of water grows louder
- Vegetation becomes denser (concealment → vulnerability)
- Camera angles shift to emphasize depth and distance

### Moving Up (Toward the Heights)
*Emotional tone: Defiance, resolve, last stand*
- Air clears; visibility expands
- Wind intensifies
- Sound of water fades
- Bare stone dominates; trees thin
- Camera angles shift to emphasize open sky and expanse

### Staying at Mid-Level (Compound)
*Emotional tone: Remembrance, everyday horror, hidden witness*
- Rhythm of daily life (water, shelter, family)
- Beauty and terror coexist
- Evidence of normalcy interrupted
- Familiar becoming alien
